📊 Program Structure

The TTA CES Physical Bootcamp is a comprehensive one-day intensive training program designed to prepare Taiwanese startups for success at CES 2026. The program features two specialized tracks tailored to different startup needs:

Challengers Track 30 startups focused on booth engagement and floor tactics
Deal Makers Track 26 startups focused on adaptive pitching and negotiation

🎯 Key Program Components

  • Morning Sessions: Three keynote presentations + Interactive cold outreach workshop + Sales pitch training across 7 stations
  • Afternoon Workshops: Specialized track-based training with rotation system ensuring comprehensive coverage
  • Closing Sessions: Cross-workshop sharing, mentor feedback panel, and actionable next steps

Workshop Details

📘 Challengers Track (30 Startups)

Workshop 1: Fact Finding & Booth Engagement
⏱ Duration: 90 minutes
👥 2 Groups (7-8 startups each)
📍 Staircase Area Left & Right
Mentors:
  • Room Left: Wilson Khoo + Luca Di Fiore
  • Room Right: Deborah Magid + Johnny Yu
Content:
  • Simulate booth interaction scenarios with mentors
  • Fact-finding techniques and discovery questions
  • 10-second hooks practice
  • Qualification and disqualification strategies
  • Handling different buyer types (distracted, hostile, perfect, wrong person)
  • Converting passerby to calendar booking
Workshop 2: CES Floor Hunting, Party Tactics & Post-Tradeshow Follow-up
⏱ Duration: 90 minutes
👥 2 Groups (7-8 startups each)
📍 Room 418 & Kitchen Area
Mentors:
  • Room 418: Bruce Bateman + Mark Birch
  • Kitchen Area: Shafeen Charania + Christopher Balmaseda
Content:
  • CES Floor Hunting (0-30 min): Target booth mapping, badge reading, route planning, booth-to-buyer strategies
  • Party Tactics (30-60 min): Getting invited to VIP events, executive approach techniques, mock party simulation
  • Post-Tradeshow Follow-up (60-90 min): 24-hour rule, cadence planning, CRM basics, multithreading, template creation

📙 Deal Makers Track (26 Startups)

Workshop 3: Adaptive Pitching
⏱ Duration: 90 minutes
👥 2 Groups (12-13 startups each)
📍 Joint Lecture → Room 302 & 308
Mentors:
  • Joint Lecture (0-20 min): Ashley Hsueh + Dr. Volker Kaese (together)
  • After Split: Ashley Hsueh (Room 302) | Dr. Volker Kaese (Room 308)
Content:
  • Pitching to different corporate positions (CEO, CFO, CTO, COO, Head of Innovation)
  • Adapting message, tone, and emphasis based on stakeholder role
  • Real-time pivoting techniques
  • Reading cues to identify decision-maker type
  • Mentor role-play with in-character feedback
Workshop 4: Meeting Skills and Negotiation
⏱ Duration: 90 minutes
👥 2 Groups (12-13 startups each)
📍 Room 301 & Counter Area
Mentors:
  • Room 301: Leland Lai (solo)
  • Counter Area: Chander Kant (solo)
Content:
  • US business meeting protocols and expectations
  • Negotiation fundamentals for enterprise deals
  • Handling objections and pushback
  • Pilot and partnership structuring
  • Give-get framework (never concede without receiving)
  • Risk reversal techniques
  • Next-step locking and calendar commitments
  • Simulated meeting scenarios: PoC discussion, partnership terms, distributor agreements

Program Objectives & Expected Outcomes

🎯 Primary Objectives

Prepare Taiwanese startups to maximize their CES 2026 experience through comprehensive training in sales, pitching, networking, and business development tactics specifically tailored for the US market and CES environment.

Key Learning Outcomes

  • 🎤 Pitch Mastery
    Deliver compelling 30-second elevator pitches and 2-3 minute sales pitches adapted to different stakeholder types (CEO, CFO, CTO, COO, Innovation Heads)
  • 🤝 Corporate Partnership Skills
    Understand how to frame solutions to de-risk innovation for large US corporations and navigate US government funding opportunities (SBIR/STTR programs)
  • 📧 Cold Outreach Excellence
    Create effective LinkedIn profiles, cold emails, and websites that speak to American business culture and lead with "what you do" not "who you are"
  • 🎪 Booth Engagement Tactics
    Master fact-finding techniques, 10-second hooks, qualification strategies, and converting passersby to calendar bookings
  • 🗺 CES Floor Strategy
    Develop target booth mapping, route planning, badge reading techniques, and booth-to-buyer conversation strategies
  • 🎉 Networking & Party Tactics
    Learn how to access VIP events, approach executives in social settings, read social cues, and maintain relationships
  • 💼 Negotiation Fundamentals
    Master US business meeting protocols, give-get framework, handling objections, and structuring pilots/partnerships
  • 📊 Follow-up Systems
    Implement 24-hour rule, 14/30/60/90-day cadence planning, CRM basics, and multithreading strategies
  • 🤖 AI-Powered Sales
    Utilize generative AI to support sales activities, outreach, and preparation for CES
