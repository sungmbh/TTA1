üí° Key Teaching Points (15-min Lecture)

  • Listen for: Pain intensity, budget authority signals ("I decide" vs "I'll pass it along"), timeline urgency
  • Close: "Can we grab 15 minutes Thursday at 2pm to show you a quick demo?" (specific ask, not "let's stay in touch")

üí° Key Teaching Points

Floor Hunting (0-10 min):

  • Prioritize booths: Target companies first, then competitor booths (learn + recruit), then tech partners
  • Badge reading: Black/VIP badges = decision-makers; scan name + company before approaching
  • Time blocking: High-traffic hours (10am-2pm) for volume; low-traffic (8-9am, 4-5pm) for quality conversations

Party Tactics (10-30 min):

  • Get invited: Ask booth contacts "Any evening events you're hosting?"; leverage mentor intros; monitor LinkedIn event posts
  • Approach: Wait for natural break in conversation; use icebreaker: "How's CES treating you so far?"
  • Exit gracefully: "I'll let you get back to your team‚Äîlet's connect on LinkedIn and grab coffee tomorrow?"

  • 24-hour rule: Email/LinkedIn within 24 hrs referencing specific conversation detail
  • Cadence: Day 1 (thank you + resource), Day 14 (case study), Day 30 (new insight), Day 60 (check-in), Day 90 (event invite)
  • CRM: Use Airtable/Notion/Google Sheets to track: Contact, Company, Pain mentioned, Next step, Follow-up date

üí° Key Teaching Points (Phase 1, 0-20 min)

5 Personas & What Each Cares About:

  • CFO: Total cost, payback period, risk mitigation, budget fit
  • CTO: Technical specs, integration complexity, security/compliance, scalability
  • COO: Implementation logistics, vendor stability, delivery reliability, change management
  • Head of Innovation: Differentiation vs competitors, future roadmap, co-development potential

  • CTO: Lead with tech architecture + security certifications
  • COO: Lead with implementation speed + support SLA
  • Innovation: Lead with unique IP + partnership model

üí° Key Teaching Points (0-15 min)

US Meeting Protocols:

  • Start with agenda: "I thought we could cover X, Y, Z‚Äîdoes that work?"
  • Be direct: Americans value efficiency; get to the point in first 2 minutes
  • End with specific next step, not "let's stay in touch"

Give-Get Framework (never concede without receiving):

  • Buyer: "Can you lower the price?" ‚Üí You: "If we adjust pricing, can you commit to a pilot start date?"
  • Buyer: "We need more proof." ‚Üí You: "If we provide a reference call with [customer], can we move to contract review?"

Objection Handling:

  • "Too expensive" ‚Üí "Compared to what? Let me show you the ROI in 6 months..."
  • "Need to see proof" ‚Üí "Great‚Äîhere's a case study. If this addresses your concerns, what's the next step?"
  • "Not a priority this quarter" ‚Üí "Understood. If I check back in 60 days, who else should I include in that conversation?"

Next-Step Locking:

  • ‚ùå Bad: "Let's stay in touch." / "Think it over and let me know."
  • ‚úÖ Good: "Can we schedule 30 minutes next Tuesday to review the proposal with your CTO?"
